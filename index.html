Kinematics: Motion in One Dimension

Definition: Kinematics is the study of motion without considering the forces that cause it.

Key Concepts:

  • Displacement: Change in position (vector quantity)
  • Velocity: Rate of change of displacement
  • Acceleration: Rate of change of velocity

Equations of Motion:

• v = u + at

• s = ut + ½at²

• v² = u² + 2as

u (initial velocity), v (final velocity), a (acceleration), s (displacement), t (time)
↓
2. List Unknown Variables
What are we solving for?
↓
3. Select Appropriate Equation

↓
4. Substitute & Solve
Plug in the values and calculate
↓
5. Check Answer
Verify units and reasonableness

⚡ Key Formulas

v = u + at (when displacement unknown)

s = ut + ½at² (when final velocity unknown)

v² = u² + 2as (when time unknown)

Average velocity = (u + v)/2

Dynamics: Forces and Motion

Definition: Dynamics studies motion considering the forces causing it.

Newton's Laws:

  • First Law: An object at rest stays at rest unless acted upon by a force
  • Second Law: F = ma (Force equals mass times acceleration)
  • Third Law: For every action, there's an equal and opposite reaction

Types of Forces:

• Gravitational Force • Frictional Force • Normal Force • Applied Force

Energy and Work

Work: Energy transferred by a force acting on an object.

W = F × d × cos(θ)

Types of Energy:

  • Kinetic Energy: KE = ½mv²
  • Potential Energy: PE = mgh
  • Mechanical Energy: Total of KE and PE

Conservation of Energy: Total energy in an isolated system remains constant.

KE = ½mv² (Kinetic Energy)

PE = mgh (Gravitational PE)

E_total = KE + PE (Mechanical Energy)

Waves and Oscillations

Wave: A disturbance that travels through space and carries energy.

Wave Properties:

  • Wavelength (λ): Distance between two consecutive crests
  • Frequency (f): Number of waves per second
  • Amplitude (A): Maximum displacement from equilibrium

Wave Equation: v = f × λ

Electromagnetic Induction (EMI)

Definition: EMI is the process of generating an electric current using a magnetic field.

Faraday's Law:

ε = -N dΦ/dt

Where ε is induced EMF, N is number of turns, and Φ is magnetic flux.

Key Points:

  • Magnetic Flux: Φ = B·A·cos(θ)
  • Lenz's Law: Induced current opposes the change in flux
  • Applications: Transformers, generators, induction motors

Laser Light & Coherence

LASER: Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation

Laser Properties:

  • Coherence: All photons in phase and same frequency
  • Directionality: Highly focused beam in one direction
  • Monochromaticity: Single pure color/wavelength

Laser Components:

  • Active Medium (gas, liquid, or solid)
  • Energy Source (pump)
  • Optical Cavity (mirrors)

Applications:

• Medical Surgery • Metal Cutting • Fiber Optics • Spectroscopy
